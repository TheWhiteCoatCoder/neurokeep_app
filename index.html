Why this matters

Dementia affects memory, attention, and executive function, and medication non-adherence can lead to missed or incorrect doses, increased hospitalizations, accelerated decline, and heavy caregiver burden.

The daily crisis

Medication adherence is one of the most immediate and dangerous pain points in dementia care.

Rigid tools break first

Traditional tools like alarms, pill boxes, and manual reminders can fail as cognitive function declines.

Families carry the load

The burden becomes emotional and logistical for caregivers and families.

MVP: reliability first

What NeuroKeep does in v1

The first version focuses on solving the core daily crisis with simple reminders, one-tap confirmation, missed-dose detection, caregiver visibility (logs and alerts), and an accessibility-first UI.

For patients

  • Simple medication reminders.
  • One-tap confirmation of intake.
  • Accessibility-first UI (large text, minimal interaction).

For caregivers

  • Caregiver visibility (logs and alerts).
  • Missed-dose detection.
  • Less uncertainty day-to-day.
Roadmap: intelligence that doesn't overwhelm

Future AI features (when the basics are rock-solid)

NeuroKeep is designed around simplicity for patients, visibility for caregivers, and intelligence that adapts, not overwhelms.

Context-aware smart reminders

Personalized reminders that adapt to routines and real-world behavior over time.

  • Context-aware smart reminders (AI personalization).
  • On-device prediction using lightweight ML models.
  • On-device inference where possible for privacy.

Beyond adherence

Support that expands into daily cognitive care once adherence is dependable.

  • Cognitive training modules (research-backed).
  • Wearable integration.
  • B2B dashboards for clinics and care facilities.

Trust, privacy, and safety

The planned platform emphasizes data minimization, encryption, on-device inference where possible, and GDPR/HIPAA-aware design.

Privacy-first

Data minimization and privacy-by-design are part of the core plan.

Secure access

Role-based access (patient/caregiver) and encrypted data storage are part of the planned stack.

Responsible AI

AI is planned as a future phase with lightweight models and on-device inference.

Early validation

The idea has been discussed with doctors and caregivers, tested as a Telegram bot prototype, presented in a university medical startup acceleration program, and refined through visits to healthcare facilities.
